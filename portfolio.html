    • School Management App

      Oct. 2022 - Sep. 2023

      Technologies: React.js, Node.js, Express.js, Sequelize, Swagger, Redux toolkit, Redis

      Collaborated as a FullStack developer within a tight-knit team, where we embarked on creating a comprehensive app tailored for schools. The application streamlines administrative tasks for teachers and school staff by offering login through a Google account linked to an Edlink (ed.link) account. This encompasses various functionalities, including class management and lesson planning, with data stored in Google Drive. More details about my work on this project

      Developed new pages and functionalities for schools, such as adding and reviewing lesson plans, implementing super user login, granting permissions to other pages, creating sortable and filterable tables, and enabling keyword searches for lesson plans. Additionally, I worked on features like synchronizing the application's database with the Edlinks database, implementing forms, pagination, enabling the addition of files from Google Drive, and incorporating YouTube videos as attachments to lesson plans. Implemented notifications for changes in lesson plans.
      Notably, this application is currently designed with a teacher-centric focus and does not include features for students.
      Tasked with developing a feature to add US state standards to lesson plans, allowing teachers to choose and link specific state standards and update them. Also, implemented a calendar feature to showcase lesson plans based on the scheduled teaching date, accommodating different time zones associated with the school.
      Implemented functionality to include schools that do not use a Learning Management System (LMS), such as Google Classroom or Canvas, into our application.

      Research

      I conducted research on implementing a calendar component to display lesson plans over periods of time. I opted for the react-big-calendar and created custom event components to represent lesson plans.

      Additionally, I was tasked with adding an attachment feature to an assignment. For this, I chose the Google Drive library. Initially, I faced a challenge as it required a key on the frontend, raising security concerns about its visibility after bundling the app. However, it turned out not to be an issue since Google Drive keys are linked to Google App Key and the domain they are used on.

      To avoid logging into Google to access the Google Drive window, I used the Google login key, which was stored in cookies after logging into the base app.

    • Scraper for LinkedIn

      Dec. 2021 - Jan. 2022

      Technologies: Django, GoogleCloudStorage, ScraperAPI

      Collaborated with a seasoned developer, functioning as a BackEnd developer on an application designed to extract important information efficiently from public LinkedIn profiles. More details about my work on this project
